Understanding STIs

What are STIs?

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs) are infections passed from one person to another through sexual contact. They can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites.

How are STIs Transmitted?

STIs are primarily spread through unprotected sexual contact, including vaginal, anal, and oral sex. Some STIs can also be transmitted through skin-to-skin contact or sharing needles.

Curable vs. Manageable STIs

Some STIs, such as chlamydia, gonorrhea, and syphilis, are curable with antibiotics. Others, like HIV and herpes, are manageable but not curable.

Getting Tested

Regular STI testing is crucial for sexual health. Many STIs can be asymptomatic, so testing helps detect infections early. Testing is available at clinics, hospitals, and at-home kits.

Talking to Your Partner

Having open and honest conversations about STIs with your partner is important. Choose a calm moment, be honest about your status, and encourage mutual testing for a healthy relationship.
